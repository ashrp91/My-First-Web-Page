Colorful Colorado

Colorado is so much more than just the rocky mountiants.Founded in 1876, Colorado is full of history! Dinosaur bones that date back to 170 million years ago,Native American Artifacts that date back to 8720 BCE, Gold and other mineral rushes! All this has helped form this beautiful state and its people and today there are some really cool places you can visit and almost feel like you are walking through time!

Bents Old Fort

Located on the Arkansas River, when it was the boarder of Mexico and the United States Bents old fort was built in 1833. This Adobe Fort was trading post for trappers, traders, travelers and native tribes who came together peacefully to trade. The fort was destroyed in 1849 due to a cholera epidemic then later designated as a national historic site in 1960 and reconstructed. Today Bents Old Fort Stands Proudly showcasing the history of our country and state. Being here it felt like traveling through time, there are even some animals like peacocks, chickens and goats roaming around the place.

Bishops Castle

Construction of Bishops Castle started in 1969 all by one man Jim Bishop and worked on the castle for 40 years. This project was a love project for Jim but didn't come without issues from Washington bureaucrats and state Government, a death of a child and cancer. All of these hardships just made Jim work harder to build what we can see, touch and tour today.

Royal Gorge Canyon

West of Canon City, Colorado as part of the Arkansas River is the Royal Gorge Canyon, Also known as the Grand Canyon of the Arkansas with the depth of 1,250 ft. This Canyon housed the Native Americans during harsh winters, Caused a 2 year company war for rail access for the local minerals of salt and lead. In 1929 the Royal Gorge Bridge was authorized to be built at 955 feet above the river which held a record of the highest bridge until 2001!
